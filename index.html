Personal Loan Singapore: The Ultimate 2026 Borrower's Guide

What Is a Personal Loan and How It Works Under Singapore's Moneylenders Act

A personal loan in Singapore is an unsecured financial product that provides borrowers with a lump sum of cash for various needs—whether covering medical emergencies, funding home renovations, consolidating debts, or managing unexpected expenses.

Unlike secured loans that require collateral, personal loans rely primarily on your income stability and creditworthiness.

The licensed moneylending industry operates under the Moneylenders Act, ensuring transparency, fair practices, and legal protection for borrowers.

When you borrow legally, you enter a regulated contract detailing your principal amount, repayment schedule, interest, and fees.

Eligibility Requirements for Singaporeans, PRs, and Foreigners

Eligibility depends on income level, residency status, and financial stability.

  • Age: Minimum 21 years old
  • Employment: Stable income source
  • Residents: NRIC required
  • Foreigners: Valid work pass required
  • Income Proof: Payslips, CPF, or bank statements

Self-employed individuals can apply with additional financial documents.

Understanding Interest Rates and Fees

Licensed lenders follow strict regulations:

  • Maximum interest: 4% per month
  • Administrative fee: Up to 10%
  • Late fee: Maximum $60/month
  • Total cost cannot exceed principal

This ensures safe and transparent borrowing.

Consequences of Late Payments

Missed payments may result in additional fees and legal recovery actions. It may also impact your credit profile.
